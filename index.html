This is an impossible target for most African countries. Just seven countries on the continent have reached the 40% target.

Botswana is the only southern African country to meet the target, largely as a result of its smaller population and a relatively developed health infrasture.

The DRC on the other hand has struggled to vaccinate its population, managing only 150 000 people, as a result of a violent political climate and logistical challenges.

Poor health infrastructure, a lack of funding for training and deploying medical staff, as well as vaccine storage issues have all played a part on why Afica is lagging behind in reaching the WHO target.
